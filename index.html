Tenant Protection

How to Avoid Rental Scams in NYC

New York City rental scams are real and on the rise. We want every tenant ‚Äî not just ours ‚Äî to be protected. Here's what to know before you sign or pay anything.

Always View Before You Pay

Never pay a deposit, security, or any fee before physically visiting (or doing a verified video tour of) the apartment. If someone pressures you to pay without seeing it, it is a scam.

Verify the Agent's License

Every real estate agent in New York must be licensed. Check any agent's license for free at the NYS Department of State Public License Search (dos.ny.gov). Brickstone Realty Group is a fully licensed NYC brokerage.

Watch for Prices That Are Too Good

If a large 2-bedroom in Manhattan is listed for $1,200/month, it is almost certainly fake. Scammers copy real listings and post them at suspiciously low prices to steal your deposit or personal info.

Avoid Wire Transfers & Cash Apps

Never pay a deposit by wire transfer, Zelle, CashApp, Venmo, prepaid debit card, or gift card. These payments are untraceable. Use a personal check or credit card, and always get a receipt.

Get Everything in Writing

Every promise made verbally should be in the lease. Read the full lease before signing. If you don't understand a clause, ask us ‚Äî we'll explain it. Never sign a lease you haven't read in full.

Protect Your Personal Information

Do not give your Social Security number, bank account details, or copy of your passport until you have verified you are working with a licensed broker and have seen the apartment in person.

Report a Scam

If you believe you have been scammed, contact the NYPD and report it to the NYS Division of Consumer Protection at 1-800-697-1220. You can also report fake listings directly to the NYC Department of Consumer & Worker Protection (DCWP) at nyc.gov/dcwp.

Your Rights

NYC Tenant Rights & the FARE Act

FARE Act (June 2025): The Fairness in Apartment Rental Expenses Act prohibits landlords from passing broker fees to tenants when the broker was hired by the landlord. If you did not hire the broker, you do not pay the broker. Brickstone Realty Group is fully FARE Act compliant.

Application Fee Cap: Under NYC law, landlords may not charge more than $20 for an application fee (which covers a credit/background check). Any amount above $20 for an application is illegal.

Security Deposit: New York State law caps the security deposit at one month's rent. Your deposit must be returned within 14 days of moving out, with an itemized list of any deductions.

No Upfront Fees: You should never be required to pay any fee before a lease is signed and a unit is secured. Non-refundable commission deposits are not permissible under New York law.

Right to a Lease: You have the right to a written lease in plain language. You also have the right to request that the lease be translated into your preferred language (Spanish, Chinese, Korean, Russian, or Haitian Creole) under NYC Admin Code.
